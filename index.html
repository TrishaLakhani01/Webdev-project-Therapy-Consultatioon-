Signs and Symptoms of Depression

Loss of Interest

Feeling a loss of interest can make it harder to do the things you need to do each day. It can leave you feeling listless, disinterested, and unmotivated to do much of anything at all.

Appetite or Weight Change

Changes in your eating habits is a symptom of depression.Loss of appetite can be an early sign of depression or a warning of a depression relapse.

Sleeping issues

People with depression may find it difficult to fall asleep and stay asleep during the night. They can also have excessive daytime sleepiness or even sleep too much.

Fatigue

Continuous feelings of sadness, anxiety, or emptiness.People with chronic fatigue syndrome usually want to engage in activities but just feel too tired to do so.

Irritatibility/Frustration

Irritability is a feature of depression itself, so it's not surprising that this form of anger is connected to depression.

Persistent feeling of sadness

Constant changes in mood, fluctuating emotional well-being, such as feeling empty, sad, hopeless.
